## What Spring yards actually need

Spring sits on the same Gulf-Coast clay belt as the rest of NW Houston. That changes how lawns hold water and how often you should be mowing.

**St. Augustine** dominates Klein and Gleannloch Farms. In August it acts like it's been ghosted — water it before it stops returning your calls. Mow at 3.5–4 inches in summer to shade the roots through dry spells.

**Bermuda** does well in the sunnier Champions and Augusta Pines lots. Mow shorter — 1.5–2 inches — and more often. It outgrows St. Augustine in heat.

**Brown patch fungus** shows up after wet falls (October–November). We look for the ring-shaped browning on every visit and flag treatment if needed. Most homeowners don't catch it until half the yard is gone.
