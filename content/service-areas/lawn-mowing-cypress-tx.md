## What Cypress yards actually need

Cypress is built on Gulf-Coast clay. That changes how lawns hold water, how grass roots, and how often you should be mowing.

**St. Augustine** is the dominant grass in Bridgeland and Towne Lake. In July and August it acts like it's been ghosted — keep it watered before it stops returning your calls. Mow at 3.5–4 inches in summer to shade the roots and hold moisture during dry spells.

**Bermuda** does well in sunnier Coles Crossing and Fairfield lots. Mow shorter — 1.5–2 inches — and more often. It outgrows St. Augustine in heat.

**Brown patch fungus** shows up after wet falls (October–November). We look for the ring-shaped browning on every visit and flag treatment if needed. Most homeowners don't catch it until half the yard is gone.
