## What Tomball yards actually need

Tomball lots vary more than most NW Houston cities. Inverness Estates is large-acre, Treeline is mid-size suburban, Cherry Pines runs older mature trees. Each needs a different mow plan.

**St. Augustine** dominates the suburban lots in Treeline and Cherry Pines. Mow at 3.5–4 inches in summer to shade roots and hold moisture. August dry stretches kill yards mowed too short.

**Bermuda + Zoysia** show up more in newer Inverness Estates builds. Mow shorter and more often — 1.5–2.5 inches.

**Mature pines + oaks** in Cherry Pines mean partial shade. St. Augustine handles shade better than Bermuda — if you have heavy tree cover and Bermuda is thinning, the grass type, not your mowing, is the issue.

**Brown patch fungus** common after fall rains under tree canopy. We flag treatment if we see the ring-shaped browning.
