Spring is here, and it's time to give your lawn the attention it deserves after a long winter. Whether you're a first-time homeowner or a seasoned gardener, these five essential tips will help you kickstart a beautiful, healthy yard this season.

## 1. Start with a Thorough Cleanup

Before you do anything else, clear your yard of winter debris — fallen branches, dead leaves, and any leftover clutter. This allows sunlight and air to reach your grass, preventing mold and disease. A good rake-through also helps loosen matted grass and promotes new growth.

## 2. Test and Treat Your Soil

Healthy grass starts with healthy soil. Pick up a soil test kit from your local garden center or extension office. The results will tell you what nutrients your lawn needs and whether the pH level needs adjusting. This small step can save you time and money by helping you apply only what your lawn actually needs.

## 3. Overseed Bare Spots

Winter can leave behind thin or bare patches. Overseeding — spreading grass seed over your existing lawn — fills in those gaps and helps create a thicker, more resilient turf. Choose a seed blend that matches your existing grass type and the sun conditions in your yard.

## 4. Set Your Mower Height Right

One of the most common lawn care mistakes is cutting grass too short. For most grass types, keep your mower blade set to about 3 inches. Taller grass shades the soil, retains moisture, and crowds out weeds. And always make sure your mower blades are sharp for a clean cut.
