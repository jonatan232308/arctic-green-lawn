# Opinions — Arctic Green

Strong takes we hold. Differentiates content from generic AI slop.

## Hard opinions

1. **Annual contracts are a trap.** Most chain lawn services lock you into 12-month commitments. We don't. If we're doing a bad job, fire us next visit.
2. **Over-watering kills more St. Augustine than drought.** Most yards in NW Houston are watered too much, not too little. Two deep waterings per week beats daily shallow runs.
3. **HOA-approved doesn't mean better.** Some of the worst yards we see have "HOA-approved" landscaping companies on contract. Look at the work, not the badge.
4. **Mulch is the highest-ROI yard upgrade.** A $200 mulch refresh holds moisture, shades roots, and makes a yard look 50% better. Underrated.
5. **Sprinkler systems should be checked twice a year.** Spring and fall. One broken head wastes more water than your whole yard uses in a week.
6. **Mowing too short is the #1 amateur mistake.** Scalping kills root depth, lets weeds in, and makes the yard browner faster.
7. **You don't need 7 different chemicals to have a good lawn.** Most yards do well on basic fertilizer + spot weed treatment + good mowing height. The rest is upsell.
8. **Local crew > national chain, every time.** We're 5 minutes away. Chain dispatchers route the closest available truck — not the one that knows your yard.

## Where these show up

- Service pages: 1 opinion as a callout box ("Why we don't lock customers into annual contracts")
- Blog posts: opening hook usually tied to one opinion
- About / homepage: 2-3 opinions as differentiators

## Hot takes we DON'T publish

- "[Specific competitor] is bad" — never name competitors negatively
- Anything political
- Pesticide debates beyond facts ("we use X because it works on Y")

## How Claude uses these

When generating a service or blog page, pick 1 opinion that fits the topic. Don't list multiple — one strong take per page. Lean into it.
