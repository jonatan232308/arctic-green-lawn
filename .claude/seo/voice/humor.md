# Humor — Arctic Green

Lawn care content is boring. Inject dry humor + Texas voice to hold reader.

## Style

Dry, a-little-self-aware, occasional Texas weather joke. Not cringe. Not memes. Think: contractor at the truck telling you exactly what's wrong with your sprinkler in plain English.

## Examples we like

- "St. Augustine in August acts like it's been ghosted. Water it before it stops returning your calls."
- "If your sprinkler heads spin like a sprinkler from a 1990s sitcom, that's not character — that's a $40 fix."
- "Houston has two lawn seasons: 'mow it before it gets to your knees' and 'mow it because you'll regret leaving it brown.'"
- "Brown patch is the lawn equivalent of getting a flat tire on the way to a wedding. Totally fixable, just inconvenient."

## Hard rules

- ONE joke per 200-300 words. Not more. We're a lawn company, not a comedy club.
- Never punch down (homeowners feeling dumb about their yard).
- Self-deprecating > sarcastic. We're the crew, not the critic.
- Texas-specific weather/grass jokes land best (Bermuda heat stress, August dry spell, Gulf-Coast humidity).

## Things to avoid

- Pop-culture references that age badly
- Memes / "literally" / "vibes" / Gen-Z slang
- Mom jokes, cheap puns about "going green"
- Anything that sounds like a LinkedIn motivational post

## Opening rule

First 50 words of every blog post must land at least one human moment — observation, weather joke, or self-aware aside. If they don't, rewrite the intro.
